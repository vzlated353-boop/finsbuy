## Types of Running Shoes

### Neutral Shoes
For runners with efficient biomechanics and normal arch height. Focus on cushioning and flexibility without corrective features.

### Stability Shoes
For overpronators who need medial support. Feature guide rails, dual-density midsoles, or posted heels to control excessive inward rolling.

### Trail Running Shoes
Designed for off-road running with aggressive lugs, rock plates, and water-resistant uppers. Sacrifice some cushioning for durability and grip.

### Racing Shoes
Lightweight with carbon fiber plates or aggressive geometry for speed. Less durable but improve running economy for race day.
