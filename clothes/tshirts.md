## Fabric Weight Guide

Fabric weight (measured in GSM - grams per square meter) affects comfort, durability, and drape:

| Weight | GSM Range | Best For | Characteristics |
|--------|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| Lightweight | 120-150 | Summer, layering | Breathable, soft, may be see-through |
| Midweight | 160-180 | Year-round | Balanced comfort and durability |
| Heavyweight | 200-220+ | Structure, streetwear | Holds shape, durable, warm |

## Types of Cotton

### Regular Cotton
Standard cotton fibers, more affordable but coarser feel. Less durable than premium options.

### Ring-Spun Cotton
Fibers are twisted and thinned, creating softer, stronger fabric. Feels smoother and lasts longer.

### Combed Cotton
Extra step removes short fibers and impurities. Even softer and more durable than ring-spun.

### Pima/Supima Cotton
Premium long-staple cotton, exceptionally soft and durable. Resists pilling and fading better than regular cotton.

## Fit Guide

### Regular Fit
Standard cut with room through chest and waist. Works for most body types. Not too tight or too loose.

### Slim Fit
Closer to body without being tight. Tapers at waist. Best for lean builds or those who prefer a modern silhouette.

### Oversized Fit
Intentionally large with dropped shoulders and extended length. Popular in streetwear. Can overwhelm petite frames.

### Boxy Fit
Wide through body with shorter length and straight cut. Creates a structured, vintage look. Works well for casual styles.
